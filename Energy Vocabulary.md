### Energy Security
A world where all people have access to reliable, affordable and responsible energy supplies that meet their needs consistently.

Example: Canada has energy security challenges because most oil/gas is in western provinces while eastern provinces import from other countries.

Related Term: Energy Independence - having enough domestic energy to meet your own needs.

### Energy Independence
When a country has enough energy resources to meet its own needs without relying on imports from other countries.

Example: Both Canada and the US have enough total energy but still require imports due to regional distribution and infrastructure limitations.

Common Misconception: Total energy production equals energy independence - transportation and infrastructure matter too.

### Energy Transition
The desire and process to move away from fossil fuels toward clean (or alternative) energy sources. Also called "energy evolution" since humans will likely use fossil fuels for the foreseeable future.

Example: Government mandate for all new vehicles to be electric by 2035.

Alternative Term: Energy Evolution - acknowledges the gradual nature of change.

### Primary Energy vs. Secondary Energy
Primary energy is energy found in nature before conversion (crude oil, natural gas, coal, uranium).  

Secondary energy is energy converted from primary sources (electricity, gasoline, diesel fuel).

Example: Natural gas from a well = primary; electricity generated from natural gas at a power plant = secondary.

Common Confusion: Electricity is always secondary energy, regardless of the primary source used to generate it.
